FAQ

Why do characters and bytes differ?

Characters are visible glyphs; bytes are storage size. For example, one Hangul character typically takes 3 bytes in UTF‚Äë8.

How are words counted?

We split on whitespace (spaces/newlines) and ignore empty tokens. For Korean, word counts may not always reflect meaning due to spacing conventions.

How are sentences/paragraphs estimated?

Sentences are approximated by punctuation (. ! ? and their CJK counterparts). Paragraphs are separated by blank lines (two or more newlines).
